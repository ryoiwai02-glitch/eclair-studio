# Operating Rules

These rules are part of the business, not decorative copy.

## V1 Scope

The first version is public-repository-only.

Do not request or accept private repositories, private ZIPs, credentials, customer data, or secrets for the v1 Mini-Audit.

## No Empty Business

Do not sell vapor, fake expertise, thin AI-generated filler, or vague secret-prompt claims.

Every paid delivery must include practical artifacts the buyer can use:

- Specific findings
- Clear recommendations
- Repo-specific templates
- Checklists
- Patches or diffs when safe and feasible
- Honest limits

## No Fraud

Do not make fake testimonials, fake usage numbers, fake customer logos, fake earnings claims, or misleading affiliation claims.

Do not imply official endorsement by OpenAI, Cursor, Anthropic, GitHub, or any other tool vendor.

## AI Disclosure

Do not pretend the audit is entirely human-made.

The service may be described as produced with an AI coding agent workflow and supervised under Eclair Studio's operating rules.

## No Secret Handling

Do not request production secrets, API keys, passwords, customer data, private identity documents, private source files, or unreleased proprietary text that is not needed for the audit.

If a customer submits secrets by mistake:

- Do not use the secret in the report.
- Do not copy the secret into logs.
- Ask for a sanitized public-repository-only submission.
- Recommend rotation or revocation if the secret may be active.

## No High-Risk Work

Do not accept work requiring legal, medical, financial, tax, safety-critical, security-incident, or regulated-system judgment.

## Honest Scope

This service improves AI-agent handoff readiness. It does not guarantee that an AI coding agent will produce perfect code, pass every test, or safely deploy to production.

## Refund Bias

For low-price early sales, resolve dissatisfaction quickly. If the delivery has no actionable value, refund instead of arguing.
